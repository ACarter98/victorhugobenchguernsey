Guernsey Attraction

This elegant bronze bench commemorates Victor Hugo's time in Guernsey, where the famous French writer lived in exile from 1855 to 1870. It was during his years at Hauteville House in St Peter Port that Hugo wrote much of his masterpiece, Les Mis√©rables.

Hugo's Guernsey

Forced into exile by Napoleon III's coup, Hugo found refuge in Guernsey. He fell in love with the island, calling it 'the rock of hospitality and liberty.' The dramatic coastline, changing weather, and relative isolation provided the perfect conditions for his creative work.

The Memorial

The bronze bench was unveiled in 2018 to mark the 150th anniversary of Les Mis√©rables. Sculpted by local artist Mark Cook, it features Hugo seated in contemplation, inviting visitors to sit beside him and share the view that inspired a literary giant.

Literary Pilgrimage

The bench has become a destination for literary tourists from around the world. Many visitors combine it with a tour of Hauteville House, now a museum dedicated to Hugo's life and work.
